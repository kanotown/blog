# The World's Biggest Sandglass
<div class="date">Aug 9, 2015 15:30</div>
<div id="post"><div id="body_show_ori">
The world's biggest sandglass is located in Nima Sand Museam,  Shimane Prefecture, Japan.<br/><br/>The sandglass was given the name <em>Suna-goyomi</em>, which means 'sand calendar.'<br/><br/>The height is 5.2 meters, the diameter of the container is 1 meter, and the diameter of the center nozzle is only 0.84 millimeters.<br/><br/>The total amount of sand is 1 ton, and it is falling at 0.032 g a second, 114 g a minute, 2740 g a day, 1 ton a year.<br/><br/>The fall speed is strictly controlled by pressure management using a computer.<br/><br/>The sandglass is turned over at 00:00 a.m, January 1st annually.<br/><br/>It was produced by Miwa professor, and the production cost was 100 million yen, the production period was three years.<br/><br/>I would like to see it directly someday.<br/><br/>By the way, the nozzle part is called <em>orifice,</em> or 'hachinokoshi(means a waist of a bee)' in Japan.
