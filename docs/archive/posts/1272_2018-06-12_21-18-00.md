<h1 id="subject_show">Sekishō Idai (積小為大)</h1>
<div class="date" hidden>Jun 12, 2018 21:18</div>
<div id="post"><div id="body_show_ori">
Yesterday, I introduced you to the Japanese term, "zayū no mei" (座右の銘), which means 'motto.'<br/><br/>Today I would like to introduce you to my zayū no mei.<br/><br/>It is "sekishō idai" (積小為大).<br/><br/>Seki (積) means 'to pile up,' shō (小) means 'small,' i (為) menas 'to accomplish,' and dai (大) means 'big.'<br/><br/>That is to say, this four-character idiom means that if you want to accomplish a great thing, it is important/necessary to pile up small efforts every day.<br/><br/>Incidentally, this idiom is said by a well known person in the Edo period, Sontoku NINOMIYA (二宮尊徳).
</div></div>
