# 【No. 2096】<strong><em>Kata no Ni ga Oriru</strong></em> (肩の荷が下りる - Taking a Load off One's Mind)
<div class="date">Jul 18, 2023 13:29</div>
<div id="post"><div id="body_show_ori">
<strong><em>Kata no Ni ga Oriru</strong></em><br/><br/>Today, I felt relieved of one's burden a little.<br/><br/>The concept of having one's burden or responsibility relieved is expressed as <strong><em>kata no ni ga oriru</em></strong> (肩の荷が下りる) in Japanese.<br/><br/>Since <strong><em>kata</em></strong> (肩) means "shoulder," <strong><em>ni</em></strong> (荷) means "burden," and <strong><em>oriru</em></strong> (下りる) means "to get off," the literal meaning of this idiom is "a burden gets off one's shoulder."<br/><br/>Here, a burden on one's shoulder is a metaphor for one's responsibility or mental burden.<br/><br/>It can be translated into English as "to take a load off one's mind."
</div></div>

<!-- more -->

<div id="post_ja"><div id="body_show_mo">
肩の荷が下りる<br/><br/>今日、私はほんの少しだけ、負担が軽くなりました。<br/><br/>責任や負担が軽くなることを、日本語で「肩の荷が下りる」と言います。<br/><br/>「肩」は "shoulder"、「荷」は "burden"、「下りる」は "to get off" を意味するので、「肩の荷が下りる」の文字どおりの意味は "a burden gets off one's shoulder" となります。<br/><br/>責任や負担を、肩に担いでいる荷物にたとえているわけです。<br/><br/>英語では "to take a load off one's mind" のように表現されます。
</div></div>
