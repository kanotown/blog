# <strong><em>Gou wo Niyasu</strong></em> (業を煮やす - Getting Irritated)
<div class="date">Aug 27, 2017 15:04</div>
<div id="post"><div id="body_show_ori">
Have you ever gotten angry because things didn't go well as you expected?<br/><br/>Getting angry like this is called "gou wo niyasu" (業を煮やす) in Japanese.<br/><br/>The word "gou" (業) of "gou wo niyasu" is a Buddhism term, meaning an act by the body, the mouth, or the mind (it especially means an act by the mind here).<br/><br/>Also, the literal meaning of "niyasu" (煮やす) means to boil something, but since the act heats something using fire, it can also mean to strengthen feelings or emotions.<br/><br/>Therefore, "gou wo niyasu" means to get angry/irritated, and the feeling is strengthened.
</div></div>

<!-- more -->

<div id="post_ja"><div id="body_show_mo">
業を煮やす<br/><br/>事が思うように運ばず、腹を立てた経験はありますか？<br/><br/>そのようなことを、日本語では「業を煮やす」と言います。<br/><br/>「業を煮やす」の「業」は仏教用語で、身、口、心による行為（ここでは特に心によるもの）を意味します。<br/><br/>また、「煮やす」の文字どおりの意味は「煮る」ことですが、火で熱することから「気持ちを激しくする」ことも表すことができます。<br/><br/>すなわち、「業を煮やす」は「怒りの気持ちが激しくなる」ことを表しているというわけです。
</div></div>
