# 【No. 1519】<strong><em>Ikken Rakuchaku</em></strong> (一件落着 - Problem Solved)
<div class="date">Feb 15, 2019 07:31</div>
<div id="post"><div id="body_show_ori">
<strong><em>Ikken Rakuchaku</em></strong><br/><br/>Today, my presentation at an academic conference was finally finished. <br/><br/>To describe such a situation, you can use the four-character idiom <strong><em>ikken rakuchaku</em></strong> (一件落着).<br/><br/><strong><em>I/ichi</em></strong> (一) means "one," <strong><em>ken</em></strong> (件) means "case," <strong><em>raku</em></strong> (落) means "to fall," <strong><em>chaku</em></strong> (着) means <em>to land,</em> so the literal meaning of this idiom is "one case falls and lands."<br/><br/>Here, <strong><em>rakuchaku</em></strong> (落着) is an idiom meaning that something is settled or resolved.<br/><br/>That is to say, <em>ikken rakuchaku</em> means that one thing/case is settled or solved.
</div></div>

<div id="post_ja"><div id="body_show_mo">
一件落着<br/><br/>今日、学会での発表が終わりました。<br/><br/>今の私の状況を表す四字熟語に、「一件落着」があります。<br/><br/>「一」は "one"、「件」は "case"、「落」は "to fall"、「着」は "to land" を意味するので、「一件落着」の文字どおりの意味は "one case falls and lands" となります。<br/><br/>ここで、「落着」はおさまりがつくことや、解決することを意味する熟語です。<br/><br/>すなわち「一件落着」とは、 ある物事が決着または解決するということです。
</div></div>
