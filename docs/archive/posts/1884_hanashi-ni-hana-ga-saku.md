# 【No. 1884】<strong><em>Hanashi ni Hana ga Saku</strong></em> (話に花が咲く - Talk and Talk)
<div class="date">Jun 24, 2021 08:46</div>
<div id="post"><div id="body_show_ori">
<strong><em>Hanashi ni Hana ga Saku</strong></em><br/><br/>Have you ever had a conversation that was so fun that you talked about many things one after another?<br/><br/>Such a situation can be described as <strong><em>hanashi ni hana ga saku</em></strong> (話に花が咲く) in Japanese.<br/><br/>Since <strong><em>hanashi</em></strong> (話) means "talk," <strong><em>hana</em></strong> (花) means "flower," and <strong><em>saku</em></strong> (咲く) means "to bloom," the literal meaning of <em>hanashi ni hana ga saku</em> is "flowers bloom on a talk."<br/><br/>Flowers bloom on a tree all at once in a short time, so by comparing a talk to the tree, this phrase was coined.<br/><br/>To mean the same thing, you can also say <strong><em>hanashi ga hazumi</em></strong> (話が弾む - literally meaning "a talk bounds"), by using the word <strong><em>hazumu</em></strong> (弾む - meaning "to bound").
</div></div>

<!-- more -->

<div id="post_ja"><div id="body_show_mo">
話に花が咲く<br/><br/>話をするのが面白く、次から次へといろいろな話をしたことはありますか？<br/><br/>そのようなことを、日本語で「話に花が咲く」といいます。<br/><br/>「話」は "talk"、「花」が "flower"、「咲く」は "to bloom" を意味するので、「話に花が咲く」の文字どおりの意味は "flowers bloom on a talk" です。<br/><br/>木に咲く花は、競うように一斉に咲き誇ることから、話を木に例えて、この表現が作られました。<br/><br/>"To bound" を意味する「弾む」を使って「話が弾む」としても、「話に花が咲く」と同様の意味になります。
</div></div>
