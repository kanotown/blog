# 【No. 2311】<strong><em>Tohoho</em></strong> (とほほ - Alas): Part 2
<div class="date">Feb 18, 2024 21:17</div>
<div id="post"><div id="body_show_ori">
<strong><em>Tohoho</em></strong>: Part 2<br/><br/>Yesterday, I introduced one of my favorite phrases, <strong><em>tohoho</em></strong> (とほほ - meaning "Alas").<br/><br/>However, note that <em>tohoho</em> has almost become a dead word, and it is rare to hear someone say that these days.<br/><br/>If you say <em>tohoho</em> unintentionally, others may have an image of you as an elderly person.<br/><br/>Incidentally, this phrase is often used by Maruko (まる子) in the famous Japanese anime <strong><em>Chibi Maruko-chan</em></strong> (ちびまる子ちゃん).<br/><br/>Therefore, despite being an elementary school student, Maruko sometimes looks like a grown-up person.
</div></div>

<!-- more -->

<div id="post_ja"><div id="body_show_mo">
とほほ Part 2<br/><br/>昨日は、私のお気に入りのフレーズ「とほほ」を紹介しました。<br/><br/>しかし、最近「とほほ」と言う人はほとんど見かけず、死語になりかけています。<br/><br/>「とほほ」とうっかり口にすると、年配の人のイメージがついてしまうかもしれません。<br/><br/>この表現は、日本国民的アニメ「ちびまる子ちゃん」で、まる子がよく発します。<br/><br/>そのためまる子は、小学生にも関わらず、ときどき大人のような雰囲気を出します。
</div></div>
