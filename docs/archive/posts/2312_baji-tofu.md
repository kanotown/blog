# 【No. 2312】<strong><em>Baji Tōfū</em></strong> (馬耳東風 - In One Ear and Out the Other)
<div class="date">Feb 19, 2024 19:40</div>
<div id="post"><div id="body_show_ori">
<strong><em>Baji Tōfū</em></strong><br/><br/>The four-character idiom <strong><em>baji tōfū</em></strong> (馬耳東風) describes a situation where someone ignores opinions, advoce, or critisism of others.<br/><br/>・<strong><em>Ba</em></strong> (馬) means "horse."<br/>・<strong><em>Ji</em></strong> (耳) means "ear."<br/>・<strong><em>Tō</em></strong> (東) means "east."<br/>・<strong><em>Fū</em></strong> (風) means "wind."<br/><br/>Therefore, the literal meaning of <strong><em>baji tōfū</em></strong> (馬耳東風) is "east wind in the horse's ears."<br/><br/>This idiom originates from a poem by the famous Chinese poet Li Bai (李白).<br/><br/>It suggests that even when the pleasant spring wind from the east into the horse's ears, the horse is not touched.
</div></div>

<!-- more -->

<div id="post_ja"><div id="body_show_mo">
馬耳東風<br/><br/>人の意見や忠告、批評などを気にせず聞き流すことを、「馬耳東風」と言います。<br/><br/>「馬」・・・ "horse"<br/>「耳」・・・ "ear"<br/>「東」・・・ "east"<br/>「風」・・・ "wind"<br/><br/>したがって「馬耳東風」の文字どおりの意味は "east wind in the horse's ears" となります。<br/><br/>この四字熟語は、中国の詩人である李白の詩の一節に由来します。<br/><br/>東から心地の良い春風が馬の耳に吹いても、馬は感動しないということです。
</div></div>
