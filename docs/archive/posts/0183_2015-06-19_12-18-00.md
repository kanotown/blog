<h1 id="subject_show">begin to / begin doing</h1>
<div class="date" hidden>Jun 19, 2015 12:18</div>
<div id="post"><div id="body_show_ori">
Today, I learned the difference between "begin to" and "begin doing."<br/><br/>According to the website, these phrases are almost same, but there are slightly different nuances.<br/><br/>"Begin to" emphasize 'beginning,' while "begin doing" emphasize 'continuation.'<br/><br/>Also, in the following cases, "begin to" is tend to be used.<br/><br/>1. When the subject is inanimate.<br/><br/>2. When the verb after the 'begin' express psychological state, such as 'see' or 'feel.'<br/><br/>3. When the 'begin' is progressive form.<br/><br/>As always, learning a little difference between nuances is difficult.
</div></div>
