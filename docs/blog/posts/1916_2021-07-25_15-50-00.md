<h1 id="subject_show">Me wo Sankaku ni Suru (目を三角にする - Getting Angry with a Stern Glare)</h1>
<div class="date" hidden>Jul 25, 2021 15:50</div>
<div id="post"><div id="body_show_ori">
Me wo Sankaku ni Suru<br/><br/>When someone gets angry and lifts the corners of his/her eyes up, it can be described as 'me wo sankaku ni suru' (目を三角にする) in Japanese.<br/><br/>Since 'me' (目) means "eye," 'sankaku' (三角) means "triangle," and 'suru' (する) means "to make," the literal meaning of 'me wo sankaku ni suru' is "to make one's eyes triangular."<br/><br/>This phrase came from the fact that when the corners of one's eyes are lifted up with fury, the eye shapes approach triangles.<br/><br/>For example, you can say 'kare wa me wo sankaku ni shite okoru' (目を三角にして怒った - ​"He got angry with a stern glare.")怒る
