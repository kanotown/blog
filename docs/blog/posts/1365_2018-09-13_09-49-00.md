Yarukoto Nasukoto<br/><br/>Yesterday, I used a Japanese expression, 'yarukoto nasukoto' (やることなすこと).<br/><br/>'Yaru' (やる) means "do," 'koto' (こと) means "thing," and 'nasu' (なす) means "do."<br/><br/>The literal meaning of this expression is "What someone does, what someone does."<br/><br/>The actual meaning of that is "All things that someone does/All of someone's attitude," and it is often used in a negative context.<br/><br/>For example, people say 'yarukoto nasukoto umaku ikanai' (やることなすことうまくいかない - Everything goes wrong).<br/><br/>On the other hand, it is sometimes used in a positive context, such as 'yarukoto nasukoto kawaii' (やることなすことかわいい - Everything she/he does is cute).
