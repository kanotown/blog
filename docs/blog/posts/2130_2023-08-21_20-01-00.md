<h1 id="subject_show">Oku no Te (奥の手 - Last Resort)</h1>
<div class="date" hidden>Aug 21, 2023 20:01</div>
<div id="post"><div id="body_show_ori">
Oku no Te<br/><br/>If you have a last resort that you do not easily show or teach others, it is called 'oku no te' (奥の手) in Japanese.<br/><br/>Since 'oku' (奥) means "inner," and 'te' (手) means "hand," the literal meaning of 'oku no te' is "inner hand."<br/><br/>Originally, 'oku' meant "left," and 'oku no te' meant "left hand."<br/><br/>In the past, Japanese people considered the left hand sacred and noble in Japan, and from this association, it came to convey the current meaning of "last resort" or "trump."<br/><br/>'Oku no te' is sometimes expressed as 'ōgi' (奥義) or 'gokui' (極意).
</div></div>
