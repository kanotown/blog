<h1 id="subject_show">Kaeru ga Naku kara Kaero (蛙が鳴くから帰ろ)</h1>
<div class="date" hidden>Sep 7, 2019 15:19</div>
<div id="post"><div id="body_show_ori">
Kaeru ga Naku kara Kaero<br/><br/>One of the most famous traditional children's songs in Japan is 'kaeru ga naku kara kaero' (蛙が鳴くから帰ろ).<br/><br/>'Kaeru' (蛙) means "frog," 'naku' (鳴く) means "call/sing/croak," 'kara' (から) is a causal conjunction, and 'kaero' (帰ろ) means "(let's) go home."<br/><br/>That is to say, the literal meaning of this song is "let's go home because frogs are croaking."<br/><br/>Frogs are a nocturnal animal that begins to croak in the evening, so the sound can be a sign for the time that childrens go home.<br/><br/>In addition, this song is also a pun -- both 'kaeru' (蛙 - "frog") and 'kaeru' (帰る - "to go home") have the same sound.
</div></div>

<div id="post_ja"><div id="body_show_mo">
蛙が鳴くから帰ろ<br/><br/>有名なわらべうたに、「蛙が鳴くから帰ろ」があります。<br/><br/>「蛙」は "frog"、「鳴く」は "call/sing/croak"、「から」は原因・理由を表す接続助詞、「帰ろ」は "(let's) go home" を意味します。<br/><br/>すなわち、この歌の文字どおりの意味は "let's go home because frogs are croaking" となります。<br/><br/>蛙は夜行性動物で、夜になると活発に鳴き始めるため、子どもが家に帰る時間の合図になり得るというわけです。<br/><br/>また、"frog" を意味する「蛙」と "to go home" を意味する「帰る」は同音であり、ダジャレにもなっています。
